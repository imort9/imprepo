What are the five most common cyber threats?

In today's digital world, we are all constantly connected to the internet. This connectivity has made our lives easier and more convenient in many ways. However, it has also made us more vulnerable to cyber threats.

Cyber threats are a real and present danger. Hackers are constantly looking for new ways to exploit vulnerabilities in our computer systems and networks in order to steal our data, disrupt our operations, or cause financial damage.

In this blog post, we will discuss the five most common cyber threats. We will also provide tips on how to protect yourself from these threats.

So, whether you are a business owner, a student, or a stay-at-home parent, it is important to be aware of the cyber threats that exist and to take steps to protect yourself.

Let's get started!

Five Most Common Cyber Threats

1. Phishing : Phishing is a type of social engineering attack in which hackers send fraudulent emails or messages that appear to be from a legitimate source in order to trick recipients into revealing personal information, such as passwords or credit card numbers.

Image: Phishing Attack

Phishing attacks are becoming increasingly sophisticated, and it can be difficult to tell the difference between a legitimate email and a phishing email. However, there are a few things you can do to protect yourself from phishing attacks.

2. Malware : Malware is a malicious software program that can be used to damage, disable, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems or networks. It can be spread through a variety of methods, including email attachments, file sharing networks, malicious websites, and drive-by downloads.

There are many different types of malware, each with its own unique purpose. Some of the most common types of malware include Viruses, Worms and Trojan horses.

Malware can be a serious threat to computer systems and networks. It can damage files, steal data, or even take control of a computer.

3. Ransomeware : A ransomware virus encrypts a victim's files and demands payment to unlock them. Recovery against ransomware may cost a significant amount of time and money.

Image: Ransomeware

Do not click on any links or open any attachments in emails that you suspect contain ransomware. Instead, forward the email to the company that it appears to be from and report it as ransomware.

4. Data breaches : Sensitive information, such as financial or personal data, is stolen from a computer system or network in a data breach.

Financial loss, identity theft, and reputational harm are just a few of the serious effects that data breaches can have on people and businesses.

5. DDos Attack : The goal of a distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) assault is to overwhelm a machine or network resource with traffic from several sources in an effort to render it unavailable to its intended users.

Hackers that employ a botnet, or a network of malware-infected machines that are under the hacker's control, frequently conduct DDoS attacks. The hacker will direct the botnet to attack the target website or server by sending orders to it. The target will subsequently be overwhelmed by the botnet's heavy traffic, rendering it inaccessible to authorised users.
